If you are one of those e-sports enthusiasts then here is a KPMG- IFSG 2019 report which could motivate you to opt for a career option in sports gaming. Why has e-sports become a viable career option in India? The KPMG- IFSG 2019 report said the rising adoption of e-sports has created an ecosystem of opportunities across the value chain. This ecosystem results being a viable career option in e-sports for people associated with the segment.
Growth factors of e-sports in India The online gaming market in India has seen tremendous growth nearly doubling the revenues over four years and reached Rs 43.8 billion in 2018, according to the report. Here are some key factors which helped to achieve it is discussed below. Rapid growth in digital infrastructure Low-cost data services and the availability of affordable smartphones have helped to increase internet penetration (43 percent as on September 2018). The report also said it resulted in large scale smartphone adoption in the country, giving a fillip to e-sports consumption. Rising disposable income and favourable demographics Due to its young median age (27.9 years in 2019), the report said India enjoys a favourable demographics for the overall growth of online gaming resulting in the rapid adoption of e-sports as well. Also, nearly 61 percent of India’s population is expected to be under the age of 35 in 2021. This relatively young population with increasing disposable incomes and access to digital infrastructure are expected to be early adopters of digital/online gaming in the country, the report claimed.
Square Enix’s Marvel’s Avengers game should’ve been the biggest slam dunk in the world. After years of teasing that gave Final Fantasy VII remake a run for its vaporware money, the AAA superhero blockbuster made its big debut at E3 2019. But instead of benefiting from the outpouring of Marvel love following Avengers: Endgame, everyone was mostly creeped out by the off-brand actors.
However, Marvel Ultimate Alliance 3 on Nintendo Switch, a game that was only announced a few months ago and is still coming out sooner, seems poised to swoop in as the Marvel universe video game you should actually care about. And while my recent hands-on session didn’t convince me the game would be a classic, at least it looks like a cool comic book game and not a bootleg MCU movie. While the title suggests this will be a follow-up to the original Marvel Ultimate Alliance game by Raven (which itself continued the legacy of the X-Men Legends games from the same developer) Marvel Ultimate Alliance 3: The Black Order is now developed by Team Ninja. And at first that sounds weird. But when you look at this frenetic beat ‘em up of colorful recognizable heroes plowing through faceless goons with huge superpowered attacks, you realize this is just another Dynasty Warriors reskin like Hyrule Warriors or Fire Emblem Warriors just with more Western appeal.
